Benefits

Pets offer unwavering emotional support through unconditional love and comfort during difficult times, providing a sense of stability and companionship. They also play a practical role in mental well-being by offering distraction from distressing symptoms and helping maintain daily routines. Beyond that, pets contribute to a person’s sense of identity and purpose, reinforcing their emotional and psychological resilience.

Human Benefits

Human-animal interactions (HAI) foster meaningful social and emotional connections by improving social attention, reducing aggression, and enhancing empathy and trust. Beyond emotional well-being, these interactions also help ease stress, naturally lowering cortisol levels, heart rate, and blood pressure, offering a calming presence during life’s most challenging moments.

Summary

Companion animals play a vital role in supporting mental health, offering comfort, stability, and a deep sense of connection. While studies show mixed quantitative results, qualitative research highlights their profound emotional, practical, and identity-related benefits. Pets provide unwavering support, ease stress, and strengthen social bonds, though challenges like financial strain must be considered. On a physiological level, interactions with animals may stimulate the oxytocin system, fostering emotional bonding and reducing stress. While further research is needed, incorporating pets into mental health care holds great promise for enhancing overall well-being.
